Evaluate the measures taken in your society to deter crime and punish criminals.

Essay Breakdown

Break the essay down into two sides:

Affirmative Negative
  • The measures taken in Singapore to deter crime and punish criminals are effective
  • What Singapore does fulfils the criteria for what we are looking for in a way to deter crime and punish criminals
  • The measures taken in Singapore to deter crime and punish criminals are not effective
  • What Singapore does not fulfil the criteria for what we are looking for in a method to deter crime and punish criminals

Keyword Subconcepts

Break the concepts in the keywords into subconcepts:

Keyword Subconcepts
Singapore
  • Conservative (especially in the past)
  • Pragmatic
  • Progressive (gradually in the present)
  • Technologically advanced
What Singapore does:
  • Our conservatism causes us to be prejudicial to criminals and crime, preferring to be safe, not sorry, by erring on the side of harshness.
  • Our pragmatism causes us to sacrifice individual liberty for collective security, letting the ends justify the means.
  • Our progressivism causes us to use education both pre-emptively and post-hoc, trusting individuals to make the right decisions.
  • Our technological prowess causes our surveillance systems to have a comprehensive and conspicuous presence across the island.
Deter crime and punish criminals
  • The justice system has the idealistic goal of promoting justice and the practical goal of protecting social order.
  • Deterrence uses the threat of unpleasant penalties to disincentivise past offenders from re-offending, and discourage potential offenders from offending.
  • Punishment additionally can be about restitution or retribution, which involve doing good to make up for the bad one has done, or suffering in proportion to the suffering one has caused.
What we are looking for in a method to deter crime and punish criminals:
  • Something that both protects social order and promotes justice.
  • Something that is both proactive and reactive.
  • Something that fulfils the justice system's responsibilities to society at large, the victim, and possibly the criminal.
  • Something that fulfils the government's broader responsibilities too, like obeying human rights and optimising resources.

Topic Sentences

Build them up into topic sentences:

  • a. The measures we take to deter crime and punish criminals are effective as our pragmatic use of draconian penalties (like hanging) defend society from danger and disorder and deliver justice to victims, fulfilling their fundamental duties to the justice system's main stakeholders. [Profundity]
  • b. The measures we take to deter crime and punish criminals are effective as our high-tech systems enforce order in both offline and online spaces, both encouraging us to behave ourselves at all times and ensuring that anyone who misbehaves gets duly disciplined. [Reliability]
  • c. The measures we take to deter crime and punish criminals are not effective as they adopt the conservative assumption that crime inherently reflects immorality and criminals are irredeemably immoral, and their harshness fails to not only be fair to people deserving a second chance but also frighten people who have given up on the system. [Profundity]
  • d. The measures we take to deter crime and punish criminals are not effective as the use of education, a more progressive approach, takes too long to bear fruit, whether to raise awareness among would-be offenders or rehabilitate ex-offenders over the course of their prison or probation term. [Timeliness]
