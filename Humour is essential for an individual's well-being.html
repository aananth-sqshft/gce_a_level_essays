'Humour is essential for an individual's well-being.' Discuss.

Essay Breakdown

Break the essay down into two sides:

Affirmative Negative
  • Humour is essential for an individual's well-being
  • Humour is highly valuable to individual well-being
  • The functions of humour match the context of individual well-being very well
  • Humour is not essential for an individual's well-being
  • Humour is not highly valuable to individual well-being
  • The functions of humour do not match the context of individual well-being very well

Keyword Subconcepts

Break the concepts in the keywords into subconcepts:

Keyword Subconcepts
Humour
  • Humour is the quality of amusing others, making them laugh
  • by doing or saying something entertaining, unexpected, or witty.
  • It can be found in an individual with a sense of humour,
  • or in media (e.g. cartoons, comedies, comics).
Individual's well-being
  • Individual well-being involves many dimensions,
  • including the emotional, physical, social, and spiritual.
  • It can be enhanced from within or from without,
  • directly by adding the good or indirectly by subtracting the bad.

Topic Sentences

Build them up into topic sentences:

  • a. Humour is essential for an individual's well-being, as it affords us the ability to laugh in the face of life's adversities and absurdities, granting us a vital emotional peace we cannot live without.
  • b. Humour is essential for an individual's well-being, as its propensity to thaw icy situations and temper tensions allows it to act as a social lubricant that we social creatures need to form meaningful relationships.
  • c. Humour is not essential for an individual's well-being, as its entertaining effects amount to mere escapism, freeing us from real-world responsibilities while problems fester, and thus do not do all that much to obliterate obstacles to our well-being.
  • d. Humour is not essential for an individual's well-being, as the health of one's physical body is determined by a multitude of factors, of which the presence or absence of laughter--a satisfying but ultimately superficial source of relief--is but one.
