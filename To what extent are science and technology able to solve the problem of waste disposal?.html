To what extent are science and technology able to solve the problem of waste disposal?

Essay Breakdown

Break the essay down into two sides:

Affirmative Negative
  • Science and technology are able to solve the problem of waste disposal to a large extent
  • Science and technology are an effective solution to the problem of waste disposal
  • What science and technology offer fulfil the criteria for what we are looking for in a solution to waste disposal
  • Science and technology are able to solve the problem of waste disposal to a small extent
  • Science and technology are not an effective solution to the problem of waste disposal
  • What science and technology offer do not fulfil the criteria for what we are looking for in a solution to waste disposal

Keyword Subconcepts

Break the concepts in the keywords into subconcepts:

Keyword Subconcepts
Science and technology
  • The product of science refers to the body of knowledge about the workings of the empirical, natural, physical world.
  • The process of science refers to a method of inquiry involving experimentation, observation, and testing of hypotheses.
  • Technology refers to the practical application of science to serve human needs and solve human problems.
What science and technology offer:
  • Science is a reliable way to probe the root cause of a problem and prescribe a solution.
  • Technology is a practical way to intervene in a problem and innovate new ways of doing things.
  • Science and technology are a resource-intensive, time-consuming solution better suited for technical, not human, problems.
Waste disposal
  • Waste is caused and created by all humans, whether at an individual level (e.g. discarded goods, leftover foods) or at a collective level (e.g. industrial byproducts).
  • Waste disposal is a problem because there is so much of it, it is harmful to both the environment and human health, and it will exist as long as human activities exist.
What we are looking for in a solution to waste disposal:
  • Something that either reduces the amount of waste, or reduces the harmfulness of waste.
  • Something that either addresses individual sources of waste or collective sources of waste.
  • Something that is universally applicable.
  • Something that is sustainable.

Topic Sentences

Build them up into topic sentences:

  • a. Science and technology are an effective solution to the problem of waste disposal as they can pinpoint the root source of excess waste and provide practical inventions to reduce how much of it we are clogging the earth's air, land, and waters with. [Profundity]
  • b. Science and technology are an effective solution to the problem of waste disposal as they can turn the issue on its head by transforming waste from harmful pollutant to helpful resource, addressing it once and for all. [Durability]
  • c. Science and technology are not an effective solution to the problem of waste disposal as they can answer only it at a technical level, but its true roots lie in human behaviour, be it individual purchases or industrial practices. [Profundity]
  • d. Science and technology are not an effective solution to the problem of waste disposal as they are costly to undertake, making them a poor fit for an issue that is global in scope--especially since the poorest nations with the greatest need to upgrade their infrastructure are also the ones least able to afford such an investment. [Reliability]
