'Online advertisements use increasingly sophisticated methods to target consumers.' To what extent does this bring more harm than good?

Essay Breakdown

Break the essay down into two sides:

Affirmative Negative
  • The increasingly sophisticated methods online advertisements use to target consumers bring more harm than good
  • Increasing sophistication in online advertisements has more of a negative impact than a positive impact
  • The disruptive features of sophisticated online advertisements change the status quo in a net negative way
  • The increasingly sophisticated methods online advertisements use to target consumers bring more good than harm
  • Increasing sophistication in online advertisements has more of a positive impact than a negative impact
  • The disruptive features of sophisticated online advertisements change the status quo in a net positive way

Keyword Subconcepts

Break the concepts in the keywords into subconcepts:

Keyword Subconcepts
Online advertisements ... increasingly sophisticated methods
  • Advertisements are media that alert the public of a product,
  • and typically promotes it and persuades people to purchase it.
  • Online advertisements exist on the internet rather than physically.
  • They utilise internet technologies, and advance alongside them,
  • increasing in the scale of data they collect,
  • the scope of how many they reach and how much they relay
  • and the speed at which they move.
  • Advertisements themselves can evolve in their techniques,
  • to become more compelling, convincing, and covert.
Harm... good
  • Effects are felt by both stakeholders on either side of the medium.
  • Those advertising are affected financially.
  • Those advertised to are affected in terms of their well-being,
  • including financially, but also mentally and morally.
  • Effects may even be felt at a societal or species-wide level
  • through the ideologies (consumerism, materialism) cultivated.

Topic Sentences

Build them up into topic sentences:

  • a. Increasingly sophisticated online advertisements do more harm than good, as the use of advanced psychological tricks to delude and deceive consumers has prompted them to part with their precious cash for products they do not need, hurting them financially much worse than it helps already-loaded firms.
  • b. Increasingly sophisticated online advertisements do more harm than good, as the power that modern technology grants firms to personalise their marketing and messaging to each consumer enables them to make money at the expense of our priceless privacy.
  • c. Increasingly sophisticated online advertisements do more good than harm, as consumers these days are evolving in media literacy at the same time as firms are enlarging their repertoire of tricks, such that we can use advertisements to make informed purchasing decisions more often than not.
  • d. Increasingly sophisticated online advertisements do more good than harm, as the use of content creators and celebrities to distance firms from the advertising act and disarm buyers has had the side-effect of rendering new forms of work profitable and possible, thus there are more stakeholders benefiting than suffering from such a trend.
