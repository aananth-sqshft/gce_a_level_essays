'Education today should involve more than the study of academic subjects.' How far do you agree?

Essay Breakdown

Break the essay down into two sides:

Affirmative Negative
  • Education today should involve more than the study of academic subjects
  • Education has a responsibility to cover academic and also non-academic subjects
  • Non-academics fall inside education's job scope
  • Education today should not / need not involve more than the study of academic subjects
  • Education has a responsibility to cover only academics / has no responsibility to cover academics and also non-academics
  • Non-academics fall outside education's job scope

Keyword Subconcepts

Break the concepts in the keywords into subconcepts:

Keyword Subconcepts
Education
  • Education is the process of transmitting knowledge and skills.
  • whether led by the individual learner or an institution.
  • Formal education, synonymous with schooling, is more systematic,
  • with planned curricula and pre-defined objectives.
What education owes its stakeholders:
  • Individuals are supposed to gain the means to navigate challenges in adult life, real life (in today's world), and working life.
  • Nations are supposed to gain well-educated workers to fill economic roles and well-socialised citizens to fill social roles.
Academic Subjects
  • Areas of study that involve classroom instruction
  • and classic book-based learning.
  • Typically contrasted with creative, practical, or technical subjects.

Topic Sentences

Build them up into topic sentences:

  • a. Education today should involve more than the study of academic subjects as the world has become so mercurial, messy, and multidisciplinary that academic exposure alone, if not coupled with an exploration of more creative fields (like art, drama, music), will not place a student in good stead to navigate its complex challenges.
  • b. Education today should involve more than the study of academic subjects as it is the best medium governments have to impart life skills to and instil lifelong values in its populace across the board and at a young age, ensuring students emerge as well-rounded adults rather than book-smart but immoral and inept children.
  • c. Education today need not involve more than the study of academic subjects as academics are the core area of responsibility of any educational institute, whereas non-academics by definition have never been and need not now be seen as their concern.
  • d. Education today should not involve more than the study of academic subjects as subjects whose essence cannot be crammed into a textbook or conveyed by a teacher are better encountered organically, outside the school walls, while institutionalising them would ruin the experience.
