Assess the view that it is always right to challenge injustice.

Essay Breakdown

Break the essay down into two sides:

Affirmative Negative
  • It is always right to challenge injustice
  • We have a responsibility to always challenge injustice / we are never justified to let injustice go unchallenged
  • We are always able to make the world a better place by challenging injustice / challenging injustice is always inside the job scope of what humans owe fellow humans
  • It is not always right to challenge injustice
  • We have no responsibility to always challenge injustice / we are sometimes justified to let injustice go unchallenged
  • We are sometimes unable to make the world a better place by challenging injustice / challenging injustice is sometimes outside the job scope of what humans owe fellow humans

Keyword Subconcepts

Break the concepts in the keywords into subconcepts:

Keyword Subconcepts
Challenge injustice
  • To challenge is stand up and step in.
  • Challenging may or may not solve the source of the problem,
  • but it always casts a metaphorical vote for what one feels is right.
  • Injustice is when a situation is unfair.
  • Fairness, in turn, is where everyone gets what they deserve,
  • which typically means either a state of equality,
  • or one where good things happen to good people and vice versa.
Right
  • Doing what is morally good.
  • May be idealistically defined, in terms of doing what one owes,
  • or more practically defined, in terms of making the world better.

Topic Sentences

Build them up into topic sentences:

  • a. It is always right to challenge injustice as humans owe it to one another, as allies from the same species and adherents of the same universal moral code, to right any wrongs we see and re-establish a world where everyone gets what they deserve. [Job Scope]
  • b. It is always right to challenge injustice as, even when the heroic move does not help things, one is still able to send a crucial message and set clear boundaries, rendering it the right move regardless of outcome. [Ability]
  • c. It is not always right to challenge injustice as stepping in to save others when one is unable to even secure one's own safety is a futile gesture that sometimes brings more harm than good. [Ability]
  • d. It is not always right to challenge injustice as meddling in matters one barely understands out of a naive desire to do good may make things worse, not better. [Ability]
