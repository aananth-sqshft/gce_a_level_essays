'There is a lack of appreciation for the value of music.' How far is this true in your society?

Essay Breakdown

Break the essay down into two sides:

Affirmative Negative
  • There is a lack of appreciation for the value of music in Singapore
  • Singapore's approach to music has had a negative impact on its level of appreciation for its value / music has low perceived value
  • The defining features of Singapore have changed the status quo in a negative way / the functions of music are not perceived to match the context of Singapore well
  • There is no lack of appreciation for the value of music in Singapore
  • Singapore's approach to music has had a positive impact on its level of appreciation for its value / music has high perceived value
  • The defining features of Singapore have changed the status quo in a positive way / the functions of music are perceived to match the context of Singapore well

Keyword Subconcepts

Break the concepts in the keywords into subconcepts:

Keyword Subconcepts
Singapore
  • Paternalistic style of governance
  • Post-materialist values
  • Pragmatic mindsets
How Singapore approaches music:
  • Something that can be engineered from the top down
  • Something that we can throw money at
What Singapore is looking for:
  • Something that meets material needs (older generation)
  • Something that meets non-material needs (younger generation)
Appreciation for the value of music
  • Appreciation can be objectively (e.g. amount of engagement)
  • or subjectively (e.g. attitude while engaging) measured.
  • The value of music comes from it being part of the arts in general,
  • or from it being about the artistic arrangement of sound in specific.
What music offers:
  • Economic benefits (e.g. careers, concerts, entertainment industry)
  • Emotional benefits for the artist, as a medium for expressing thoughts and externalising feelings, and for the listener, through harmonious melodies and heartfelt lyrics.
  • Sociocultural benefits (e.g. NDP songs)

Topic Sentences

Build them up into topic sentences:

  • a. There is a lack of appreciation for the value of music in pragmatic Singapore, as we only care about material, usually monetary forms of value to the exclusion of everything else, causing music's emotional benefits to go underappreciated.
  • b. There is a lack of appreciation for the value of music in paternalistic Singapore, as efforts to induce a love for music in citizens or introduce music into the cultural scene have felt either half-hearted or heavy-handed, thereby turning us off the artistic field instead.
  • c. There is no lack of appreciation for the value of music in post-materialist Singapore, as we are beginning to care about higher-order needs like a serene, stable, and stress-free mental state too, bringing music's value into the spotlight at last.
  • d. There is no lack of appreciation for the value of music in pragmatic Singapore, as we are realising that music can be a lucrative source of economic gain and regarding it as being valuable after all.
